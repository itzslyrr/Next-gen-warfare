# 🇰🇵 Armory: North Korea

**Doctrine snapshot:** a small arsenal engineered for one purpose — survivable retaliation that makes preemption impossible to guarantee. The program's pace is the story: from first ICBM test (2017) to solid-fuel ICBMs (2023) in six years.

## Headliners in this knowledge base

| System | Class | Signature capability |
|---|---|---|
| [Hwasong-18](../inventory/hwasong-18.md) | Solid-fuel ICBM | Launch-on-minutes, ~15,000 km |

## Also notable (not yet profiled)

- **Hwasong-17** — the huge liquid-fueled "monster missile," largest road-mobile ICBM anywhere
- **Hwasong-19** — Hwasong-18's even larger successor, tested October 2024
- **KN-23** — maneuvering quasi-ballistic SRBM (Iskander-pattern); reportedly used in Ukraine
- **Hwasong-8 / Hwasong-16B** — claimed hypersonic glide vehicle tests
- **Pukguksong series** — submarine-launched ballistic missiles

## Reading the arsenal
Pyongyang systematically copies the survivability playbook of larger powers: mobile launchers, solid fuel, canisterization, and claimed MIRV/HGV work — each step removing a window that adversaries once counted on. Sanctions have slowed but demonstrably not stopped the roadmap.
