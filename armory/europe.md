# 🇪🇺 Armory: Europe

**Doctrine snapshot:** two independent nuclear deterrents (UK, France) plus a conventional precision-strike culture built on air-launched cruise missiles — the systems that, alongside US types, have defined the Ukraine war's deep-strike campaign.

## Key systems

| System | Country | Class | Notable |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Trident II D5](../inventory/trident-ii-d5.md) | 🇬🇧 UK (US-built) | SLBM | Entire UK deterrent, *Vanguard*-class subs |
| M51 | 🇫🇷 France | SLBM | Wholly independent French triad leg, ~10,000 km |
| ASMP-A | 🇫🇷 France | Air-launched supersonic | Airborne leg of French deterrence, Rafale-carried |
| Storm Shadow / SCALP-EG | 🇬🇧/🇫🇷 | Stealth cruise | Combat-proven in Ukraine, ~250-560 km |
| Taurus KEPD 350 | 🇩🇪/🇸🇪 | Cruise | Deep-penetration two-stage warhead, ~500 km |
| Exocet | 🇫🇷 | Anti-ship | The classic sea-skimmer, combat record since 1982 |

## In development

- **ELSA / Land Cruise Missile** — European long-range strike initiative launched 2024 (France, Germany, Italy, Poland+)
- **FC/ASW (Future Cruise & Anti-Ship Weapon)** — Franco-British Storm Shadow/Exocet successor, subsonic-stealth + supersonic pair
- **HYDIS² / Aquila** — MBDA-led European counter-hypersonic interceptor concepts

## Reading the arsenal
Europe's strength is precision conventional strike and sea-based deterrence rather than raw scale. Ukraine turned Storm Shadow/SCALP from shelf items into the continent's most consequential weapons — and exposed the range/stockpile gaps that the new joint programs aim to close.
