# 🇨🇳 Armory: China

**Doctrine snapshot:** the fastest-growing strategic arsenal in the world. The PLA Rocket Force fields the broadest missile *spectrum* anywhere — from short-range conventional to ICBM — with a signature specialty: anti-ship ballistic missiles designed to hold US carriers at arm's length ("anti-access/area denial").

## Headliners in this knowledge base

| System | Class | Signature capability |
|---|---|---|
| [DF-41](../inventory/df-41.md) | Road-mobile ICBM | Up to 10 MIRVs, 12–15,000 km |
| [DF-17](../inventory/df-17.md) | Hypersonic glide | First HGV fielded at scale |

## Also notable (not yet profiled)

- **DF-21D / DF-26** — the "carrier-killer" anti-ship ballistic missiles; DF-26 dual-capable at 4,000 km ("Guam Express")
- **YJ-21** — ship-launched hypersonic anti-ship ballistic missile
- **JL-3** — new SLBM arming Type 094/096 submarines
- **FOBS test (2021)** — orbital bombardment demonstration with a hypersonic glider; went most of the way around the planet
- **New silo fields** — 300+ silos under construction in the western deserts, the visible face of a projected 1,000+ warhead arsenal by 2030

## Reading the arsenal
China builds for a specific scenario: keeping US carrier groups and regional bases out of a Western Pacific fight. Everything from DF-17's glide profile to the ASBM family serves that geometry — while the ICBM buildout signals the shift from "minimum deterrence" to peer status.
