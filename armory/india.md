# 🇮🇳 Armory: India

**Doctrine snapshot:** declared No First Use posture built on "credible minimum deterrence" — a compact but rapidly modernizing arsenal, plus one of the world's most successful missile export stories in BrahMos.

## Headliners in this knowledge base

| System | Class | Signature capability |
|---|---|---|
| [Agni-V](../inventory/agni-v.md) | ICBM-class | MIRV demonstrated 2024 (Mission Divyastra) |
| [BrahMos](../inventory/brahmos.md) | Supersonic cruise | Fastest cruise missile in wide service; exported |

## Also notable (not yet profiled)

- **Agni-P** — new-generation canisterized MRBM, technology testbed for the Agni line
- **K-4 / K-5** — SLBMs for the *Arihant*-class submarines, completing India's triad
- **BrahMos-II** — planned hypersonic successor (scramjet, Mach 7-class, linked to Zircon technology)
- **HSTDV** — indigenous scramjet demonstrator, the seed of India's hypersonic program
- **Ballistic Missile Defence Programme** — two-tier indigenous interceptor system (PAD/AAD)

## Reading the arsenal
India runs a two-track program: a deliberate, treaty-unconstrained strategic build (Agni + submarine leg) aimed at China deterrence, and an aggressive precision-strike/export track led by BrahMos. The 2024 MIRV test moved it into a club of five.
