# 🇷🇺 Armory: Russia

**Doctrine snapshot:** the largest nuclear arsenal on Earth, paired with an explicit strategy of fielding "defense-evading" novel systems — hypersonic gliders, nuclear-powered cruise missiles, and heavy ICBMs — announced as a package by President Putin in 2018.

## Headliners in this knowledge base

| System | Class | Signature capability |
|---|---|---|
| [RS-28 Sarmat](../inventory/rs-28-sarmat.md) | Heavy ICBM | ~18,000 km, 10t payload, polar trajectories |
| [Avangard](../inventory/avangard.md) | Hypersonic glide | Mach 27 claimed, in service since 2019 |
| [Zircon](../inventory/zircon.md) | Hypersonic cruise | First operational sea-launched HCM |
| [Kinzhal](../inventory/kinzhal.md) | Air-launched ballistic | First "hypersonic" fired in combat |

## Also notable (not yet profiled)

- **Kalibr** — the workhorse ship/sub-launched cruise missile, heavily used in Syria and Ukraine
- **Iskander-M** — quasi-ballistic SRBM, the land-based counterpart to Kinzhal
- **RS-24 Yars** — the mainstay mobile ICBM force
- **Burevestnik** — experimental nuclear-*powered* cruise missile with claimed unlimited range; test record contested
- **Poseidon** — nuclear-powered autonomous torpedo/underwater drone

## Reading the arsenal
Russia's pattern: compensate for conventional and economic disadvantages with strategic novelty. Nearly every "first" in the hypersonic era (first HGV in service, first HCM at sea, first combat use) is Russian — though combat results in Ukraine have tempered the marketing.
