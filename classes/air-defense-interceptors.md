# Class: Air Defense & Interceptors

**The shield side of the ledger.** Every offensive system in this knowledge base is shaped by what these systems can and cannot do.

## The layered model

| Layer | Engages | Representative systems |
|---|---|---|
| Exo-atmospheric midcourse | ICBM warheads in space | GMD (US), SM-3 (US/Japan), Arrow-3 (Israel) |
| Upper-tier terminal | Ballistic warheads in late descent | THAAD (US), S-500 (Russia) |
| Lower-tier terminal | Ballistic + aircraft + cruise | **Patriot PAC-3** (US), S-400 (Russia), David's Sling (Israel) |
| Point defense | Cruise missiles, drones, close threats | Iron Dome (Israel), Pantsir (Russia), CIWS guns |

## Scorecard against each offensive class

- **vs. [Ballistic missiles](ballistic-missiles.md):** feasible but expensive; MIRVs and decoys strain interceptor inventories — the attacker's warheads are cheaper than the defender's interceptors.
- **vs. [Cruise missiles](cruise-missiles.md):** solvable with enough sensors; the challenge is coverage — low flyers hide behind terrain, and saturation salvos exhaust magazines.
- **vs. [Hypersonics](hypersonic-weapons.md):** the open problem. Quasi-ballistic [Kinzhal](../inventory/kinzhal.md) has been intercepted by PAC-3 in Ukraine; true glide vehicles ([Avangard](../inventory/avangard.md), [DF-17](../inventory/df-17.md)) currently have no proven counter. The US Glide Phase Interceptor and space-based tracking layers are the announced responses.

## Key concepts

- **Cost-exchange ratio** — a $4M interceptor vs. a $50k drone is a losing trade; economics, not physics, is often the binding constraint.
- **Sensor fusion** — space-based infrared tracking is the announced answer to hypersonic gliders that terrestrial radars lose over the horizon.
- **Proven in combat** — Ukraine and the Red Sea (2023–24) turned air defense from a theoretical discipline into the most combat-tested military technology of the decade.
