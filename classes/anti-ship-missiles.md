# Class: Anti-Ship Missiles

**The problem they solve:** a moving warship is a hard target — it relocates, jams, decoys, and shoots back with layered defenses. Anti-ship missiles answer with sea-skimming approach, terminal maneuvering, and increasingly, speed.

## Key concepts

- **Sea-skimming** — flying meters above the waves keeps the missile under the ship's radar horizon until the final ~20–30 km.
- **The reaction-time equation** — a subsonic skimmer gives defenders ~2–3 minutes from detection; Mach 3 cuts it to ~30 seconds; Mach 8 to seconds.
- **ASBM (anti-ship ballistic missile)** — China's DF-21D pioneered dropping a maneuvering warhead onto a moving carrier from a ballistic arc; the hardest variant to defend against and the reason carrier operating patterns changed in the Pacific.
- **Kill chain over warhead** — finding and tracking a ship across an ocean (satellites, OTH radar, drones) is as decisive as the missile itself.

## Countered by
Shipboard layered defense: long-range SAMs → point-defense missiles → close-in guns → decoys/EW. See [Air Defense & Interceptors](air-defense-interceptors.md).
