# Class: Cruise Missiles

**Flight profile:** essentially small, disposable, unmanned jet aircraft. They fly *low* — often under 50 m — using terrain to hide from radar, navigating by GPS, inertial guidance, and terrain/scene matching to strike within meters.

## Speed tiers

| Tier | Speed | Trade-off | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Subsonic | ~Mach 0.7 | Max range & stealth, long flight time | [Tomahawk](../inventory/tomahawk.md), Kalibr, Storm Shadow |
| Supersonic | Mach 2–3 | Speed compresses reaction time; less range | [BrahMos](../inventory/brahmos.md), Oniks |
| Hypersonic | Mach 5+ | See [Hypersonic Weapons](hypersonic-weapons.md) | [Zircon](../inventory/zircon.md) |

## Key concepts

- **Terrain masking** — flying below radar horizon means defenders may get only seconds of track time, even against a slow missile.
- **Salvo saturation** — cruise missiles are (relatively) cheap; doctrine fires them in coordinated volleys from ships, subs, and aircraft simultaneously ([Tomahawk](../inventory/tomahawk.md)'s signature use).
- **In-flight retargeting** — modern variants (Tomahawk Block V) receive new targets mid-flight via datalink.

## Strengths & weaknesses

✅ Precision, low-altitude stealth, launch-platform flexibility, affordability at volume
❌ Slow ones give layered defenses many shots; dependent on navigation aids that can be jammed

## Countered by
Point-defense guns, short/medium-range SAMs, fighters, and electronic warfare — see [Air Defense & Interceptors](air-defense-interceptors.md).
