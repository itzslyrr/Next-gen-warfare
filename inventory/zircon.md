## Overview
Zircon is a scramjet-powered cruise missile — it breathes air and sustains hypersonic speed through the whole flight rather than only during a dive. Fired from standard Russian 3S-14 vertical launch cells, the same tubes that carry Kalibr and Oniks, it gives frigates, corvettes, and submarines a weapon that crosses 400+ km in a handful of minutes. At Mach 8+, defenders may have well under a minute of usable reaction time after detection over the radar horizon.

## Why it matters
- **First sea-launched hypersonic cruise missile** declared operational anywhere.
- **Reaction-time compression:** its speed collapses naval air-defense engagement windows.
- **Plasma stealth side-effect:** the ionized sheath around the missile at speed complicates radar tracking and its own seeker design alike.
