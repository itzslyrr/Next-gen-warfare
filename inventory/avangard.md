## Overview
Avangard is a boost-glide weapon: a ballistic missile lofts it to the edge of space, after which the wedge-shaped vehicle detaches and *glides* through the upper atmosphere at extreme speed, maneuvering laterally and in depth along the way. Unlike a traditional warhead falling on a predictable arc, an HGV's flight path is low, fast, and steerable — the combination that makes it exceptionally hard for existing missile-defense radars and interceptors to track and engage.

## Why it matters
- **First operational HGV** — Russia declared it combat-ready in 2019, ahead of peers.
- **Defense-evasion archetype:** its maneuvering glide profile is the textbook case that spurred US and Chinese counter-hypersonic programs.
- **Delivered by heavyweights:** designed to ride on the [RS-28 Sarmat](rs-28-sarmat.md).
