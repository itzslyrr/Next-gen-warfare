# Hwasong-18 (화성-18)

| Quick facts | |
|---|---|
| **Origin** | 🇰🇵 North Korea |
| **Class** | Road-mobile [ICBM](../classes/ballistic-missiles.md), **solid-fuel**, canisterized |
| **Range** | ~15,000 km (estimated from lofted test apogees) |
| **Speed** | ~Mach 24 terminal |
| **Status** | Tested April, July, December 2023; paraded in numbers; successor Hwasong-19 tested Oct 2024 |

## Overview
The Hwasong-18 marked North Korea's jump from liquid-fueled ICBMs — which need hours of visible, vulnerable fueling before launch — to solid fuel, which can fire within minutes of an order. Test flights on lofted trajectories reached apogees above 6,000 km, implying range sufficient to cover the continental United States on a standard trajectory. Its three-stage, cold-launched, canisterized design closely follows the pattern of Russia's Topol-M and China's [DF-41](df-41.md).

## Why it matters
- **Solid fuel = no warning window:** removes the pre-launch fueling signature that watchers relied on.
- **Survivability leap** for a small arsenal: mobile, sealed, and fast to fire.
- **Trajectory of the program:** each iteration (17 → 18 → 19) has closed the gap with established ICBM powers faster than most forecasts.
